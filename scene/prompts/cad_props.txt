GLOBAL PROP INSTRUCTIONS

Render every prop as a reusable product asset.

Use clean CAD-inspired industrial product photography.

White seamless studio background.

Orthographic 3/4 perspective.

Neutral studio lighting with soft shadows.

Maintain exact dimensions, proportions and engineering details described in the prop prompt.

Do not stylize, cartoonize or simplify the objects.

Preserve all mounting holes, fasteners, connectors, cables, sprockets, bearings and visible mechanical interfaces.

Do not invent additional components.

Do not omit specified components.

Do not merge multiple props into a single object unless explicitly requested.

When a prop contains multiple pieces, arrange them neatly as an exploded product layout while preserving their relationships.

All labels, displays and markings must be simple and generic unless explicitly specified.

No people.

No hands.

No arms.

No bodies.

No faces.

No clothing.

No gloves.

No environmental backgrounds.

No workshop scenes.

No dramatic lighting.

No cinematic effects.

No depth-of-field blur.

No motion blur.

No dirt, scratches or weathering unless explicitly requested.

Keep the same appearance, color and geometry every time the same prop appears throughout the project.

If uncertain, prioritize engineering consistency over artistic interpretation.
